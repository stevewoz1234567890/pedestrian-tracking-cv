The moving camera scenario presented several unique challenges that required specific modifications: \\

\textbf{Additional Challenges:} \\

1. \textbf{Camera Motion}: The camera itself is moving, creating apparent motion for all objects in the scene \\
2. \textbf{Scale Changes}: Objects appear larger/smaller as the camera moves closer/farther \\
3. \textbf{Background Motion}: Everything in the scene appears to move relative to the camera \\
4. \textbf{Target Selection}: Need to identify and track a specific person (man with hat) among multiple detections \\

\textbf{Modifications Applied:} \\

1. \textbf{Robust Target Selection}: \\
   - Used largest detection on first frame to identify the target \\
   - Implemented distance-based association to maintain target identity \\
   - Added fallback to select closest detection if target is lost \\

2. \textbf{Enhanced Kalman Filter Tuning}: \\
   - Increased process noise to handle camera motion \\
   - Adjusted measurement noise for moving camera scenarios \\
   - Used larger initial uncertainty to account for camera dynamics \\

3. \textbf{Detection Association}: \\
   - Implemented minimum distance matching between detections and tracker \\
   - Added logic to handle cases where target is temporarily lost \\
   - Used position prediction to guide detection association \\

4. \textbf{Visualization Improvements}: \\
   - Added text labels to identify the tracked person \\
   - Used distinct colors for tracking boxes \\
   - Implemented fallback visualization when target is lost \\

\textbf{Key Differences from Previous Sections:} \\

- \textbf{Dynamic Background}: Unlike static camera scenarios, everything moves \\
- \textbf{Scale Adaptation}: Target size changes significantly as camera moves \\
- \textbf{Target Persistence}: Need to maintain target identity across frames \\
- \textbf{Robustness}: Must handle temporary occlusions and detection failures \\

\textbf{Technical Solutions:} \\

1. \textbf{Adaptive Detection}: Used HOG detector with multiple scales to handle size changes \\
2. \textbf{Prediction-Based Association}: Used KF predictions to guide detection matching \\
3. \textbf{Fallback Mechanisms}: Implemented recovery strategies when target is lost \\
4. \textbf{Scale-Invariant Tracking}: Adjusted detection parameters for moving camera \\

The moving camera scenario required a more robust tracking system that could handle the additional complexity of camera motion while maintaining target identity and adapting to scale changes. The combination of HOG detection with Kalman filtering proved effective for this challenging scenario.
